02 · Mock Campaign

Campaign brief:
Meetup

A self-directed case study — not commissioned by Bending Spoons — built to show how I'd approach content strategy for one of your products, following Meetup's 2024 rebrand and the launch of its free organiser plan.

Product: Meetup · Self-initiated
Objective

Turn scroll-stoppers into first RSVPs

Drive first-RSVP conversions among 18–27 year-olds who discover Meetup through short-form video, using the free organiser plan as a hook to also grow the organiser side of the marketplace.

Audience Insight

Fear of being perceived, not fear of connecting

Solo attendance isn't blocked by a lack of desire for connection — it's blocked by fear of how it looks. People worry that showing up alone reads as "loner" rather than "brave." Watching someone else go through it first, honestly, lets viewers rehearse that fear from a safe distance — by the time they consider going themselves, they already know what to expect and that the payoff is worth it.

Content Concept

"Alone Together"

A UGC-led series that shows the discomfort on screen instead of editing it out — proof the fear passes, told by people who felt it. Four content pillars:

01 The Anxious Arrival — raw POV from the car park to the door, nerves visibly intact
02 Why Coming Alone Isn't Weird — real attendees say plainly why they came solo and what actually happened
03 Organiser spotlights — the person behind the event, tied to the new free plan
04 The Debrief — honest reflection after, naming the specific payoff gained
Why This, Not That

Rejected the polished testimonial

Considered a straightforward testimonial format — happy attendees speaking to camera about how great Meetup is. Rejected it: polished testimonials read as ads, and this audience is specifically distrustful of anything that looks like it's selling them confidence. Visible discomfort is what makes the reassurance credible.

Risk & Mitigation

Discomfort must resolve, not linger

Risk: showing real anxiety on screen could discourage rather than reassure if a clip doesn't clearly land on a payoff. Mitigation: every piece of content follows the same arc — nerves shown, then explicitly resolved — so the fear is never the last thing the viewer sees.

Format & Cadence

Built for the feed, not the brand page

3 posts/week across TikTok + Reels, native captions and pacing, creator-shot rather than studio-shot. Paid boosting behind the top organic performer each week rather than a fixed media plan.

If The Numbers Underperform

If view → profile-visit rate comes in low, the hook isn't landing in the first 2 seconds — test alternate opening frames before touching anything else. If profile → RSVP click is weak but views are strong, the content is being watched but not trusted enough to act on — that points to a bio-link or landing-page problem, not a content problem.
